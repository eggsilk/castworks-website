**Architecture:** Single long-scroll landing page. Multi-page site stays on the roadmap for post-revenue (once case studies and SEO clustering justify it).

**Narrative sequence (locked):** Hook → Agitation → Paradigm Shift → Methodology → Risk Reversal → Authority → Terminal CTA.

**Positioning anchor:**
- Target: small-to-mid-sized companies who've tried creator marketing and hit walls — databases that don't convert, agencies priced for enterprise.
- Offer: full-service creator campaigns — **strategy + discovery + delivery** — on pure commission.
- Mechanism (internal, not pitched): AI tooling collapses discovery/vetting cost, so commission-only economics work below agency scale.
- Hero term: **creator**. SEO/meta term: **influencer**.
- Visual language: "Pragmatic Premium" — editorial restraint + methodology visualizations + high-contrast data modules.

## 2 — AGITATION (The problem)

**Heading:** You've already tried this.

**Body:**

Most small-to-mid-sized companies doing creator marketing today face two options. Both are broken.

**Databases ask you to be your own agency.**
Creator databases sell you a filtered list and expect you to do the rest: pitch every creator, qualify the fit, negotiate the deal, coordinate the placement, measure the outcome. What you get is a lead list, not a campaign. Every hour of filtering is an hour you don't have.

**Agencies ask you to pay like you're an enterprise.**
Traditional creator marketing agencies run on retainers sized for eight-figure marketing budgets. If yours is smaller, you're either priced out — or treated as a secondary account while the agency's real clients get the attention. The retainer gets paid whether the campaign converts or not.

Most companies in the middle run sporadic one-off campaigns, hire a freelancer, or quietly give up on the channel. None of it compounds.

## 3 — PARADIGM SHIFT (The solution)

**Heading:** The third option.

**Body:**

CastWorks is a full-service creator marketing operation — strategy, discovery, delivery, reporting — run on pure commission. You pay when deals close. Nothing before. Nothing on retainer. No platform fee.

That math only works for us because our internal infrastructure collapses the expensive parts of the work. Discovery that would take a team of sourcers weeks, we run in hours. Vetting that would require a sourcing analyst per creator runs through our own tooling. The operational overhead that forces every other agency into retainer pricing, we've engineered out.

What's left is the part you can't automate — strategy, judgment, relationship work. That stays human. The whole package fits in a budget a real mid-market company can sustain.

## 4 — METHODOLOGY (How a campaign runs)

**Heading:** How a campaign runs.

**Sub-heading:** Four steps from brief to close. Typically two to four weeks, end to end.

**01 — Strategy**
You tell us your product, your audience, your goals. We translate that into a campaign brief: which audience segment, which creator archetype, what kind of placement, what budget shape. If creator marketing isn't the right channel for your goal, we say so before anything moves.

**02 — Discovery**
Our internal tooling identifies creators whose audiences align with your brief. Candidates are ranked on engagement quality, audience overlap, commercial history, and content fit — where other tools stop at follower count, we evaluate fit. Every match is reviewed by hand.

**03 — Package**
You receive a ranked shortlist with per-creator rationale — what they make, who watches, what they've run before, why they fit. You pick the bundle.

**04 — Close**
We handle outreach, negotiate contracts, coordinate placement, and track results. You approve the final package. We close it. You pay on deals that ship.

**Heading (sub-section):** What you don't do.

Sourcing. Vetting. Outreach. Contracts. Reporting. Those are our job. Your job is the brief and the approval.

**[Design note for Phase 2:** each of the four steps paired with a small line-art glyph. Not decorative — each glyph should depict the actual work of that step.]

## 5 — RISK REVERSAL (Why commission)

**Heading:** Why commission is the point.

**Body:**

Most agencies charge retainers because forecasting hours across clients gives them predictable cashflow. The trade-off is that they get paid the same whether your campaign converts or doesn't. That's a bad bet for you.

Commission-only inverts it. We make money when you make money. We don't take campaigns we can't close. We don't stretch a campaign across more hours than it needs. The incentive is aligned by construction.

This only works because our internal tooling compresses the cost of getting to a shippable campaign. Other agencies can't sustainably offer commission-only pricing — the hours add up faster than the commissions can cover. We've engineered the math out. That's the entire business model.

**Three zeros that matter:**
- $0 retainer
- $0 platform fee
- $0 minimum spend

A commission on deals we close, together.

**[Design note for Phase 2:** "Three zeros" block is the one place on the page that should use a high-contrast dark data module — borrows the SaaS pricing-table visual language to signal tech-enablement, without showing a product UI.]

## 6 — AUTHORITY (Manifesto + founder)

**Heading:** Why this exists.

**Body (first-person, founder voice — essay rhythm):**

Creator advertising is growing four times faster than total media spend. By 2025, it will clear $37B globally. Every number says it's one of the most efficient channels for companies trying to reach specific audiences.

And yet creator marketing has effectively been reserved for enterprise budgets. Not because the creators aren't there — the creators are everywhere. But because nobody has built the infrastructure to serve anyone below enterprise scale.

There's a structural reason. Running a creator campaign end-to-end — scoping, sourcing, vetting, outreach, contracts, reporting — takes real hours. At enterprise budgets, an agency can absorb those hours per client. At mid-market budgets, they can't. So the mid-market gets two leftover options: software you have to operate yourself, or agencies that don't really want your business.

CastWorks fixes the math. Our internal tooling collapses the cost of discovery and vetting by orders of magnitude. What's left is the work that can't be automated — and that stays human. The result is an agency-grade service priced at a commission a real mid-market company can sustain.

The channel shouldn't be a luxury anymore. That's what we're building.

**— Ege Ipek, Founder**

## Notes for Phase 2 design

- **Visual hierarchy of text:** extreme size contrast between headlines and body (72–96px hero headline, 18px body). Every section heading is plain-language — design should carry the weight, not decoration.
- **Imagery strategy:** no abstract "floating orb" gradients. The three visual moments on the page are: (a) a line-art topology diagram in Section 3, (b) four line-art glyphs in Section 4, (c) a dark high-contrast "three zeros" data module in Section 5. Everything else is pure typography and whitespace.
- **"Influencer" appears only in SEO/meta.** "Creator" everywhere on-page.
- **CTA persistence:** "See if you qualify" button appears in hero, and again sticky-reappearing after the risk-reversal section. Not on every section.
- **Mobile:** stack the two-column Agitation block vertically. Keep the essay block (Section 6) at the same measure on mobile — don't stretch.
- **Founder byline**: tested, not anonymized. Face photo optional, but only if it's professional quality. No stock.
- **Reference sites for visual cues:** Anthropic (tone) · Animalz (editorial discipline) · Refine Labs (contrarian confidence) · Wynter (hero boldness) · Acquire.com (trust engineering) · Directive Consulting (methodology visualization).
